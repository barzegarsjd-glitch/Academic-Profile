About Me

I am a PhD student at the School of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences at Ulster University, where my research focuses on advanced drug delivery systems and biomaterials.

I hold both a Master of Science and a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering from Shiraz University, Iran. During my academic studies, I developed a strong foundation in transport phenomena, thermodynamics, and materials science, graduating with high distinction (M.Sc. GPA: 18.50/20, B.Sc. GPA: 18.16/20).

My academic journey has been driven by a deep interest in applying engineering principles to biomedical challenges, particularly in the development of polymeric micro/nanomaterials and targeted drug delivery systems for treating diseases such as cancer and infections.

Research Interests

Biomaterials: Bridging Biology and Engineering

Biomaterials are specially designed materials—either natural or synthetic—used to interact with biological systems for medical purposes such as repairing, replacing, or enhancing body functions. Common examples include polymers, metals, ceramics, and composites used in implants like artificial joints, dental fillings, and tissue scaffolds. The key goal of biomaterials is biocompatibility, meaning they must perform their function without causing harmful reactions in the body. Advances in biomaterials are driving innovations in regenerative medicine, drug delivery, and biomedical engineering, improving both the quality and longevity of human life.

Drug Delivery: Targeting Treatment Effectively

Drug delivery refers to the methods and technologies used to transport therapeutic substances in the body to achieve optimal treatment outcomes. It focuses on delivering drugs at the right place, time, and dosage while minimizing side effects. Modern drug delivery systems include controlled-release tablets, nanoparticles, liposomes, and transdermal patches, all designed to improve the efficiency and precision of treatments. Advances in this field are enhancing the effectiveness of therapies for diseases such as cancer, diabetes, and infections, making treatments safer and more patient-friendly.

Tissue Regeneration: Restoring the Body Naturally

Tissue regeneration is the process of repairing or replacing damaged or diseased tissues using the body’s own healing mechanisms, often supported by advanced biomedical techniques. It involves the use of stem cells, growth factors, and biomaterial scaffolds to stimulate new tissue formation and restore normal function. This field plays a crucial role in regenerative medicine, offering promising solutions for conditions such as organ failure, severe injuries, and degenerative diseases. Ongoing research in tissue regeneration aims to create more effective therapies that can fully restore damaged tissues and improve patients’ quality of life.
